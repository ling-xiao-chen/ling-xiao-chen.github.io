I am broadly interested in nationalism, political culture, public opinion formation, and authoritarian politics. My dissertation examines how nationalism and popular culture become entwined in China's entertainment sector: the state enlists celebrities to amplify nationalism, and audiences interpret these cues and draw boundaries of national belonging. This project draws on mixed methods, including in-depth interviews, a national survey experiment, and archival research.
